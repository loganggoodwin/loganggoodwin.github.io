Overview

This project presents a comprehensive Information Assurance (IA) Plan designed to protect organizational information assets against modern threats. I like framing IA as a sustainable security program—not a checklist— aligned to confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA).

Problem Statement

Organizations face increasing exposure to ransomware, credential compromise, and exploitation of unpatched systems. Inconsistent control enforcement and limited centralized monitoring increase overall risk.

My Approach

1) IA Posture Assessment (CIA)

  • Confidentiality: RBAC and limited MFA, but inconsistent enforcement
  • Integrity: patching and monitoring existed but were applied unevenly
  • Availability: backups existed but DR testing was limited

2) Risk Assessment (NIST SP 800-30)

  • Structured evaluation of threat likelihood, business impact, and control gaps
  • High-risk areas: unpatched vulnerabilities, ransomware, phishing/credential compromise

3) Policy & Control Design

  • Incident response and escalation standards
  • Disaster recovery and business continuity policy
  • Access control + MFA enforcement aligned to least privilege
  • Logging, monitoring, and continuous improvement cadence

Key Deliverables

  • Information Assurance Plan
  • Risk matrix with likelihood/impact ratings
  • Defined roles and responsibilities
  • IR and DR policy foundation
  • Maintenance and review strategy

Frameworks & Standards Used

  • NIST SP 800-30 — Risk Assessment
  • NIST SP 800-53 — Security & Privacy Controls
  • NIST SP 800-61 — Incident Response
  • NIST SP 800-34 — Contingency Planning / DR
  • CIA Triad

Outcome & Impact

  • Improves detection/response readiness and reduces dwell time
  • Strengthens accountability through defined roles
  • Improves resilience against ransomware and outages
  • Aligns security posture with recognized best practices

What I Would Improve Next

  • Centralized SIEM integration for real-time monitoring
  • Automated patch management and configuration baselines
  • Tabletop + live incident response exercises
  • Security awareness training with phishing simulations
