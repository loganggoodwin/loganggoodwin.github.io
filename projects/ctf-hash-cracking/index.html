Reflection

Defensive takeaways.

  1. Evidence beats assumptions. The strongest answers came from verifying artifacts, logs, packets, hashes, or visible clues instead of relying on first impressions.
  2. Team performance depends on communication. In a broad CTF, the team has to divide categories, avoid duplicate effort, and share findings clearly.
  3. Completion matters, but accuracy matters too. The 92.75% completion rate shows broad coverage, while the accuracy score is a reminder that careful validation is part of security work.
  4. CTF practice translates to real operations. OSINT, log review, traffic analysis, scanning, and credential analysis are all useful in incident response, help desk escalation, and junior security roles.
