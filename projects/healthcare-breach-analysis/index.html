Healthcare Data Breach Analysis

Legal, ethical, regulatory, and operational analysis of the Trinity Health incident

ProjectsHome

This project analyzes the Trinity Health data breach through the lenses of confidentiality, integrity, availability, and accountability. It connects technical gaps to HIPAA obligations, patient trust, legal exposure, and the broader cultural impact of healthcare breaches.

Core findings

  • Unauthorized access persisted for nearly a month before detection.
  • Sensitive patient data including Social Security numbers and treatment information was exposed.
  • The breach raised concerns around monitoring, incident response, and patient notification quality.
  • Regulatory and legal issues extended beyond compliance into ethics and trust.

Standards and frameworks discussed

  • HIPAA Security Rule and Breach Notification expectations.
  • FTC guidance around reasonable security practices.
  • NIST SP 800-53 and HITRUST as stronger external security baselines.
  • International privacy implications through GDPR-style expectations.

Recommendations

  • Improve intrusion detection and continuous monitoring.
  • Use multi-factor authentication for privileged systems.
  • Strengthen role-based awareness training.
  • Adopt more transparent and patient-centered breach communication.

Why this project matters

I think this project is valuable because it demonstrates that I can analyze incidents beyond the technical symptoms. It connects cybersecurity to privacy, law, ethics, and organizational responsibility.
