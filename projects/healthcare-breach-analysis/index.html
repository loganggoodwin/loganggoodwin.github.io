Overview

This project analyzes a real-world healthcare data breach to show how failures in cybersecurity controls, monitoring, and governance can produce major legal, ethical, and societal consequences. I evaluate the incident through the lens of confidentiality, integrity, availability, and accountability.

Why it matters: In healthcare, cybersecurity decisions directly affect patient trust and can create long-term organizational risk.

Incident Summary

A healthcare organization’s email systems were compromised for an extended period, exposing approximately 21,000 patient records. Data types included names/addresses, dates of birth, Social Security numbers, and medical information.

Security Failures Identified

Detection & Monitoring

  • Limited continuous monitoring enabled attacker persistence
  • Weak centralized logging reduced visibility into abnormal activity

Access Controls

  • Insufficient authentication protections increased credential compromise risk
  • Lack of multi-factor authentication for sensitive systems

Incident Response

  • Delayed detection increased dwell time and exposure
  • Insufficient response readiness reduced containment speed

Legal & Regulatory Impact

  • HIPAA Security Rule: administrative, technical, and physical safeguards
  • Breach notification obligations: timelines and content requirements
  • Reasonable security expectations: aligned to industry guidance and common controls

Ethical Considerations

  • High duty of care for protecting sensitive patient information
  • Need for transparent post-incident communication
  • Extended exposure increases identity theft and personal harm risk

Standards & Framework Alignment

  • HIPAA Security Rule
  • NIST SP 800-53 (controls)
  • NIST SP 800-61 (incident response)
  • ISO/IEC 27001 (ISMS)

Recommendations

  • Implement continuous monitoring (SIEM-based detection)
  • Enforce MFA for privileged and sensitive access
  • Test incident response and recovery procedures regularly
  • Security awareness training with measurable outcomes
  • Adopt a recognized framework beyond minimum compliance

Skills Demonstrated

  • Incident and breach analysis
  • Healthcare regulatory awareness
  • Cyber law and ethics
  • Risk assessment and mitigation
  • Communicating technical risk to non-technical stakeholders
