Information Assurance Plan

NIST-aligned governance, access control, monitoring, response, and continuity planning

ProjectsHome

This project establishes a structured information assurance plan to protect confidentiality, integrity, and availability across an organization facing modern cyber threats. It is built around risk assessment, clearly defined roles, incident response, disaster recovery, and continuous maintenance.

What the project covers

  • CIA triad alignment across people, process, and technology.
  • Security roles and responsibilities for leadership, IT, and end users.
  • Risk assessment focused on patching, credential compromise, logging, backups, and ransomware.
  • Policy statements for incident response, disaster response, access control, and plan maintenance.

Major weaknesses identified

  • Delayed and inconsistent patch management.
  • Logging and monitoring that lacked centralization.
  • Limited disaster recovery testing and response validation.
  • Infrequent awareness training against phishing and credential theft.

High-priority recommendations

  • Enforce multi-factor authentication for administrative and sensitive access.
  • Centralize logging and continuous monitoring.
  • Test incident response and disaster recovery procedures regularly.
  • Apply least privilege and formal access reviews.
