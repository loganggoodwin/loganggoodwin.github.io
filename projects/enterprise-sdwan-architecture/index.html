Enterprise Network Analysis and SD-WAN Architecture

SD-WAN · NGFW · QoS · Disaster Recovery · SolarWinds · Splunk

ProjectsHome

This project evaluates the existing network infrastructure of SNHUEnergy, Inc., a multi-site energy company with offices in Dallas and Memphis, identifies performance and security deficiencies, and proposes a dual-hub SD-WAN architecture to support expansion into Kansas City and Houston. It covers OSI-layer analysis, traffic characterization, security hardening, and a phased implementation plan.

Executive summary

I believe this project is one of the stronger examples in the portfolio because it connects infrastructure design to business continuity. The proposed architecture removes single points of failure, improves remote and interoffice connectivity, and introduces security and monitoring controls that scale with additional locations.

Proposed architecture

  • Dallas and Houston operate as redundant SD-WAN hub sites, with Memphis and Kansas City as spokes.
  • Each site uses dual WAN links, with SD-WAN steering traffic across primary and backup paths.
  • End-to-end IPsec VPN tunnels protect intersite traffic, while SSL VPN supports secure remote access.
  • Cloud-bound traffic can break out intelligently rather than backhauling everything through a single hub.

LAN segmentation and security controls

  • Departmental VLAN separation for Finance, HR, and Operations.
  • Layer 3 switching with 802.1Q trunking and QoS tagging.
  • Next-generation firewalls at each site with application-layer inspection, IPS, and geo-blocking.
  • Multi-factor authentication for remote and cloud access.
  • Monitoring through SolarWinds for performance visibility and Splunk for centralized log correlation.

Disaster recovery and resiliency

Houston serves as a secondary hub for failover, with replicated core services such as Active Directory, DNS, and database workloads. This design reduces recovery time after a site outage and supports continued operations even when a provider or primary site becomes unavailable.
